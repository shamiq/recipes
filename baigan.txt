1 large brinjal/eggplant/bhartha baingan
1 medium onion, finely chopped
1 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
5 to 6 medium garlic cloves, slice them
1 green chili/hari mirch, chopped
¼ tsp red chili powder/lal mirch powder [optional]
1/8 tsp turmeric powder/haldi
1/4 tsp garam masala
1/4 tsp cumin seeds
1 tsp grated ginger
2 tbsp coriander leaves, finely chopped
1 to 2 tbsp oil
A pinch of asafoetida
Salt as required


1. if using oven, preheat to 450.
2. make 3 slits in eggplant, place garlic inside.
3. wrap in foil, and cook for ~1hr, checking at 45 for doneness (blunt knife will travel through easily)

4. peel, discarding skin, and mash thoroughly
5. In a karahai/wok heat oil on medium flame.
6. add cumin seeds and asafoetida. (30s)
7. Stir and add chopped onion. Saute onion for 2-3 minutes or until very light brown.
8. Now add green chili, 1 tbsp green coriander and ginger, mix well and cook for another 30 seconds.
9. Throw in the dry masala – turmeric powder, red chili, garam masala and salt for 45s.
10. add in tomatoes. Lower the flame and cover the karahi. Let the tomatoes cook completely and become mushy.
11. Once tomatoes are cooked through, add the mashed eggplant and saute well for 3-4 minutes on medium flame.
12. Check for seasoning (salt/black pepper) and adjust to your taste.
